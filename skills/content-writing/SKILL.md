# Content Writing

> Write human-facing content — blog posts, announcements, newsletters, copy that sounds human.

## When to use

Use this skill when you need content for humans to read: blog posts, announcements, newsletters, marketing copy, or any text that shouldn't sound AI-generated.

## Before writing

Always discuss with the user first:
- Who is the audience?
- What tone (professional, casual, playful, authoritative)?
- What length (sentences, paragraph, full article)?
- Where is it going (blog, website, email, social)?
- Do you have existing writing samples to match the voice?

## Writing principles

- **Write for humans**: Imagine a specific person reading this. Use natural rhythm and sentence variation.
- **Sound human, not AI**: Avoid em-dash spam, relentless transitions (furthermore, moreover), perfectly balanced clauses.
- **Right length, not maximum**: Say what needs to be said and stop. Don't pad.
- **Closely collaborate**: Share drafts early. Ask for feedback. Offer alternatives.
- **Avoid lazy abstractions**: "Streamline your workflow" → "save 10 minutes on every deploy"
- **Match the project's voice**: Read existing content first to absorb the voice.

## What to avoid

- "In today's digital landscape", "delve into", "navigate the complexities", "unlock the power of"
- More than one em-dash per paragraph
- Emojis in non-casual content
- Overuse of transition words
- Perfectly balanced sentence structures every time
- Generic content that could apply to any project
- Excessive politeness or hedging
